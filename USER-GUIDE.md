## 1. Getting Started - Sign Up

- ✅ Secure encrypted storage

**If you received a referral link:**
- The link will look like: `tagmything.com/auth?ref=ABCD1234`
- Follow the same signup steps above
- **Bonus**: You and your referrer both earn extra credits!

## 4. Asset Tagging

### Understanding the Tagging Process

TagMyThing uses a **dual-location system**:
1. **Manual Location**: Where the item is physically stored
2. **Automatic GPS**: Where you are when using the app (if enabled)

### Step-by-Step Tagging

**Step 1: Start Tagging**
- Click **"Tag Now"** from dashboard or navigation
- Grant camera permissions when prompted
- Choose **Photo** (25 credits) or **Video** (60 credits)

**Step 2: Capture Your Item**
- **Photo Tagging**:
  - Frame your item clearly
  - Ensure good lighting
  - Tap the capture button
  - Review and retake if needed
- **Video Tagging**:
  - Record up to 60 seconds
  - Show the item from multiple angles
  - Include any serial numbers or unique features
  - Tap stop when finished

**Step 3: Add Item Details**
- **Title** (Required): Give your item a clear name
- **Description**: Add detailed information, stories, or significance
- **Categories/Tags**: Choose relevant categories for easy searching
- **Estimated Value**: Optional monetary value for insurance purposes
- **Manual Location**: Enter where the item is physically stored
  - Examples: "Kitchen drawer", "Home office safe", "Car glove compartment"

**Step 4: Location Information**
- **Manual Location**: Text field for where the item is stored
- **Automatic GPS**: If enabled, app captures where YOU are when tagging
- **Privacy**: GPS coordinates are only stored if you've given permission

**Step 5: Review & Save**
- Review all information for accuracy
- Check that credits will be deducted (25 for photo, 60 for video)
- Click **"Save Asset"**
- Confirmation that item is now protected

### Best Practices for Tagging

**Photography Tips:**
- Use good lighting (natural light is best)
- Capture serial numbers, model numbers, unique markings
- Take multiple angles for valuable items
- Include size references (coin, ruler) for jewelry

**Video Tips:**
- Start with overview, then zoom to details
- Speak while recording to add context
- Show any documentation (receipts, certificates)
- Keep videos under 60 seconds for faster upload

**Information Tips:**
- Be descriptive but concise
- Include purchase date, price, store
- Add emotional significance or family history
- Use consistent naming for easy searching

TagMyThing offers **two types of location data**:

1. **Manual Item Location**: Where the tagged item is physically stored
2. **Automatic Session GPS**: Where you are when using the app

**During Signup:**
- Location Permission Modal appears after account creation

**From Dashboard:**

**Browser Permissions:**
- Your browser will ask for location access

### How GPS Works

**When GPS is Enabled:**
- App tracks your location while you use it
- Coordinates are captured automatically when tagging assets

- Google Maps provides human-readable addresses

**When GPS is Disabled:**
- No automatic location capture
- Manual location fields still work normally
- Enhanced privacy with no coordinate tracking
- You can re-enable anytime from dashboard

### Privacy & Control

**Your Data:**
- GPS data is only collected with your explicit permission
- Location data is encrypted and securely stored
- Only you can see your location information
- You can disable GPS tracking anytime

**Usage:**
- Location improves asset organization and searching
- Helps with insurance claims and theft recovery
- Provides context for when/where items were documented
- Never shared with third parties

## 6. Next of Kin & Legacy Planning

### Understanding the Next-of-Kin System

TagMyThing's **Dead Man's Switch** system ensures your belongings are passed to trusted people automatically.

### Key Concepts

**Incoming NOK**: Assets where someone else has named you as their Next of Kin
**Outgoing NOK**: Assets where you've named someone else as your Next of Kin
**Dead Man's Switch (DMS)**: Time-based system that grants access after inactivity
**Privacy Protection**: NOK sees assignments but not asset details until triggered

### Setting Up Next of Kin

**Step 1: Add Trusted People**
1. Go to **Dashboard > Next of Kin**
2. Click **"Add Next of Kin"**
3. Enter their information:
   - Full Name
   - Email Address
   - Phone Number
   - Relationship (spouse, child, sibling, etc.)
   - Notes (optional)

**Step 2: Assign Assets**
- **Individual Assignment**: Choose specific assets for specific people
- **Mass Assignment**: Assign all your assets to one person at once
- **Multiple NOK**: Different items can go to different people

**Step 3: Set Dead Man's Switch Period**
Choose how long you must be inactive before access is granted:
- **1 Year**: For urgent situations
- **2 Years**: Standard recommendation
- **3 Years**: More conservative approach
- **4-5 Years**: Maximum privacy protection

### How Assignments Work

**For You (Assigning Assets):**
1. **Create Assignment**: Choose asset and Next of Kin
2. **Set Timeline**: Select DMS period
3. **Privacy**: NOK gets notification but can't see asset details
4. **Monitoring**: System tracks your login activity
5. **Trigger**: If inactive for set period, NOK gets access

**For Your NOK (Receiving Assets):**
1. **Notification**: Email notification they've been assigned
2. **Privacy**: Can see they're assigned but not asset contents
3. **Waiting Period**: No access until DMS triggers
4. **Access Granted**: Full access to assigned assets after DMS
5. **Reassignment**: Can pass responsibility to someone else

### Managing NOK Assignments

**Dashboard Overview:**
- **Incoming Count**: Assets assigned to you
- **Outgoing Count**: Assets you've assigned
- **Triggered Assignments**: DMS has activated
- **Upcoming DMS**: Assignments nearing trigger date

**Modifying Assignments:**
- Change NOK assignments anytime
- Update DMS periods
- Add or remove assets from assignments
- Cancel assignments completely

**Reassignment Feature:**
- NOK can reassign their responsibilities
- Useful if circumstances change
- Maintains family flexibility
- Preserves original asset protection

### Family Communication

**Best Practices:**
- Discuss your wishes with chosen NOK
- Explain how the system works
- Update assignments as family changes
- Review assignments annually

**What to Tell Your NOK:**
- They've been chosen as Next of Kin
- How long they'll wait for access
- What types of assets they'll receive
- How to contact TagMyThing if needed
